Burruss from afar

Burruss Hall is one of main centerpieces of Virginia Tech and includes 3003 seat auditorium for major events. Inside the building, it also holds classrooms for the College of Architecture and Urban Studies.

Marching Cadets

The Corp of Cadets currently have 1127 members within the organization. Each member must wear a specific uniform for each day of the week.

Fall in session

Throughout the campus of Virginia Tech, there are wonderful flowers which display an array of colors. This is a picture taken outside of Burruss Hall where the flowers brighten up the display of the campus.

The flag fly high

Lane Hall can be founded in between the corp of cadets residence halls and contains offices for disciplines in the College of Liberal Arts and Human Sciences. The American flag in this shot stays up throughout the day.

Torg Bridge: The entrance to Virginia Tech

Torgerson Bridge is a centerpiece on the campus of Virginia Tech and serves as an entrance to the campus on Alumni Hall. The bridge connects Torgerson Hall and Newman Library.

Never Forget

The 4/16 Memorial is located outside of Burruss Hall and is a place of remembrance for the thirty-two Virginia Tech students who lost their lives on April 16th, 2007.

Slusher stands tall

Slusher Hall is one of the largest on-campus residences and holds up to 330 male and female students every academic year. There are two sections of Slusher Hall: Slusher Tower and Slusher Wing.

THe values of Virginia Tech

The pylons at Virginia Tech represent the eight values of the university. In front of the pylons, it also reads the university's motto Ut Proism or better known as "That I May Serve"

A place of Christ

The War Memorial Chapel is located under the pylons on the campus and its doors face the far east side of the Drillfield. The chapel will hold many services and masses throughout the week and weekends.
